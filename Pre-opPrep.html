Why You Need This Surgery

Based on your medical records and the symptoms you reported (recurrent abdominal pain, especially after meals, and ultrasound showing gallstones), you need this surgery to remove your gallbladder. The gallbladder stores bile that helps digest fats, but gallstones can block bile flow, causing pain, inflammation, and potential complications like infection or pancreatitis.

How the Surgery Will Be Performed

You will have laparoscopic surgery, which means the surgeon will make several small cuts (incisions) in your abdomen instead of one large cut. Through these small openings, they will insert a tiny camera and special surgical tools to carefully remove your gallbladder.

What to Expect During Surgery

  • You will receive general anesthesia, so you'll be asleep and won't feel any pain during the procedure.
  • The surgeon will inflate your abdomen with gas to create space to see and work.
  • Using the camera as a guide, the surgeon will locate, separate, and remove your gallbladder.
  • The surgery typically takes 1-2 hours.

After the Surgery

You will wake up in the recovery room where nurses will monitor you. Most patients go home the same day or after one night in the hospital. You may have some shoulder pain from the gas used during surgery, but this usually goes away in 1-2 days. You can return to normal activities within 1-2 weeks.

Benefits of This Approach

  • Less pain after surgery compared to open surgery
  • Smaller scars that heal better
  • Shorter hospital stay
  • Faster return to normal activities
  • Lower risk of infection
