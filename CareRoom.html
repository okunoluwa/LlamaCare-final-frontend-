Wound Care Instructions

Important: These are general guidelines for natural wound healing support. This is not clinical advice. Always follow your surgeon's specific instructions and contact them with any concerns.

Proper wound care is essential for healing. Here are organic, natural approaches to support your body's healing process:

  • Keep it clean and dry: Gently clean around the wound with mild soap and water. Pat dry with a clean towel.
  • Allow air circulation: When safe to do so, let the wound breathe without dressings for short periods.
  • Nutrition support: Eat foods rich in vitamin C (citrus fruits, bell peppers), zinc (nuts, seeds), and protein to support tissue repair.
  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water to help your body flush toxins and deliver nutrients to healing tissues.
  • Rest adequately: Your body heals during sleep. Aim for 7-9 hours of quality sleep each night.
  • Avoid irritants: Don't apply creams, oils, or natural remedies unless approved by your healthcare provider.
  • Hand hygiene: Always wash your hands before touching or cleaning your wound area.
  • Wear loose clothing: Avoid tight clothing that might rub against or irritate the wound area.

Red flags requiring medical attention: Increased redness, swelling, warmth, pus, foul odor, fever, or increased pain. Contact your healthcare provider immediately if you notice any of these signs.
